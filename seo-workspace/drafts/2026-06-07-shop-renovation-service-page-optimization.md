## 中文页面建议文案

### 页面定位

这不是泛泛介绍“开店设计趋势”的文章，而是给准备咨询、比价或排期开工的客户看的服务页。页面需要回答：

- 你们能做哪些店铺与商业空间装修内容？
- 报价和工期为什么差别这么大？
- 开店前应先确认哪些审批、动线、展示与收纳问题？
- 下一步应该怎样安排现场测量与报价？

### 建议开头文案

FLASH CAST 提供店铺装修与 retail fit-out 服务，覆盖 Kuala Lumpur、Selangor 与 Klang Valley。服务可包含现场测量、店面布局规划、展示区与柜台设计、收纳与后场动线、照明与饰面协调、材料建议、报价整理、施工管理与交付前检查。

如果你正在规划零售店、餐饮门店、展示厅、诊所或小型商业空间翻新，建议先确认使用需求、顾客动线、展示重点、机电条件和开业时间，再进入报价与施工安排。

### 建议主体结构

#### 1. 适合哪些客户

适合以下类型客户：

- 零售店与 shoplot 经营者
- 餐饮门店、咖啡店与外带窗口
- 诊所、展示厅、美容与服务型门店
- 需要配合 landlord、mall management 或 local approval 的商业客户

#### 2. 店铺装修通常包含哪些内容

可根据项目范围包含：

- 现场测量与需求沟通
- 顾客动线与展示布局规划
- 柜台、展示柜、后场收纳与工作区规划
- 门头、招牌、照明与饰面协调
- 机电点位、空调、排风或设备位协调
- 报价说明、材料建议与施工排期
- 施工管理与交付前检查

#### 3. 开店前常见问题

- 货架、展示台与收银区位置会直接影响顾客停留与购买路径。
- F&B、零售、展厅和诊所的重点不同，不能只套同一种平面。
- 商场或业主管理规则、可施工时段、押金和文件要求会影响开工顺序。
- 门头、灯光、后场储物和员工操作区若规划太晚，通常会压缩开业准备时间。

#### 4. 预算与工期因素

不写虚假的固定价格。建议只解释真实变量：

- 店铺面积与现状
- 是否需要门头、招牌、柜台、展示系统
- 灯光、空调、排风或设备位调整
- 材料、饰面、定制木作与五金
- landlord / management 审批与施工时间限制
- 开业日期倒排造成的交期压力

工期不承诺固定天数。建议说明会根据现场情况、审批、材料交期和施工范围确认排期。

#### 5. 规划示例与边界

可加入明确标注的 `设计方案 / rendering concept / planning example`，例如：

- 小型零售门店前区展示墙 + 收银台 + 后场收纳布局示意
- 餐饮外带门店柜台与等候区规划示意
- 展示厅的展示墙、灯带与顾客行走路线示意

这些内容必须标注为规划示例，不得描述成真实完工案例。

#### 6. 商业空间参考说明

可把 `Kota Damansara Clinic Reception Fit-Out` 用作商业空间流程与饰面协调参考，但不要写成真实零售店案例。正确说法应是：

- 可参考已公开商业空间项目的施工协同方式
- 不把诊所项目包装为店铺案例

## 英文页面建议文案

### Positioning

This is not a generic article about retail design trends. It should function as a commercial service page for owners comparing contractors, timelines, approvals, and fit-out scope before opening.

### Suggested opening copy

FLASH CAST provides shop renovation and retail fit-out services across Kuala Lumpur, Selangor, and Klang Valley. The scope can include site measurement, layout planning, display zones, counters, storage, lighting coordination, material advice, quotation breakdown, project coordination, and pre-opening checks.

If you are planning a retail shop, F&B outlet, showroom, clinic, or service-based outlet, it helps to confirm customer flow, display priorities, M&E requirements, and your opening schedule before finalizing the fit-out scope.

### Suggested body structure

#### 1. Who this service is for

- Retail shop owners and shoplot operators
- F&B outlets and takeaway concepts
- Showrooms, clinics, beauty, and service-based spaces
- Businesses working around landlord, mall management, or local approval requirements

#### 2. What a shop renovation can include

- Site measurement and project brief
- Customer flow and display planning
- Counter, display cabinet, and back-of-house storage planning
- Facade, signage, lighting, and finish coordination
- M&E, air-conditioning, exhaust, or equipment-point coordination
- Material advice, quotation breakdown, and work sequencing
- Project coordination and pre-opening inspection

#### 3. What owners often need to decide before opening

- How the storefront, cashier area, and display path should support customer movement
- How storage and staff circulation should work behind the counter
- Whether landlord or mall rules affect signage, working hours, or deposit requirements
- Which items need to be prioritized first if the opening deadline is tight

#### 4. Budget and timeline factors

Avoid fixed price claims. Explain variables such as floor area, facade and signage scope, display systems, counters, lighting, M&E coordination, landlord rules, material lead times, and opening deadlines.

#### 5. Planning examples and rendering concepts

Use clearly labeled `design concept`, `rendering concept`, or `planning example` blocks, such as:

- a compact retail storefront with cashier counter and product wall
- a takeaway-oriented service flow for a small F&B outlet
- a showroom plan with lighting emphasis and visitor circulation

These must be labeled as conceptual planning material, not completed real projects.

#### 6. Commercial fit-out reference note

`Kota Damansara Clinic Reception Fit-Out` can be cited only as a commercial fit-out workflow and finish-coordination reference, not as a real retail case study.
